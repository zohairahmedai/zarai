Why ZarAI?

Addressing critical gaps in agricultural technology access for Pakistan's farming communities

Pakistan's Agriculture Challenges

Pakistan's agriculture sector employs over 38% of the labor force, yet small farmers lack access to timely, accurate crop advisory. Most existing systems are English-only, text-based, and unsuitable for rural communities.

Lack of Localized Digital Advisory

Current agricultural AI tools do not support Urdu, ignore local crop varieties, and fail to address the specific pest and disease pressures of Pakistan's agro-ecological zones.

AI for Small Farmers

ZarAI bridges this gap by delivering a multilingual, multimodal advisory system that understands local context, supports voice and image input, and provides actionable guidance in the farmer's own language.

About the Project

Research motivation, problem statement, and societal impact

Research Motivation

Agriculture is the backbone of Pakistan's economy, contributing approximately 19% to GDP. However, small-scale farmers — who constitute over 60% of farming households — face severe challenges including pest outbreaks, disease identification difficulties, and limited access to expert advisory services. The existing extension services are underfunded and unable to reach millions of farmers in remote areas.

Research Problem

There is no comprehensive AI-powered agricultural advisory system that combines multilingual conversational AI (Urdu + English), image-based crop disease diagnosis, and retrieval-augmented knowledge delivery in a single platform tailored for Pakistan's agricultural context.

Expected Societal Impact

ZarAI aims to democratize agricultural knowledge access, reduce crop losses through early disease detection, improve farmer decision-making, and contribute to food security — directly supporting UN SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) and SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure).

Proposed System Architecture

Complete pipeline from data collection to multimodal advisory deployment

Phase 1 — Data Collection

  • Crop disease image datasets (wheat, rice, cotton, tomato, maize)
  • Agricultural knowledge documents from Pakistan agricultural research sources
  • Bilingual (Urdu-English) agricultural query corpus development
  • Expert-validated Q&A pairs for training and evaluation

Phase 2 — RAG Knowledge Base

  • Document chunking with semantic-aware splitting strategies
  • Dense embeddings using multilingual sentence transformers
  • FAISS vector database for efficient similarity search
  • Hybrid retrieval combining dense and sparse methods

Phase 3 — LLM Chatbot

  • Qwen/Llama model integration with agricultural domain adaptation
  • Multilingual chatbot supporting Urdu and English conversations
  • Translation pipeline for seamless language switching
  • Context-aware response generation with RAG grounding

Phase 4 — Computer Vision

  • Crop disease classification using fine-tuned vision models
  • Multi-class image analysis for pest and disease identification
  • Severity assessment and progression tracking
  • Integration with knowledge base for treatment recommendations

Phase 5 — Multimodal Integration

  • Combined image + text interaction pipeline
  • Visual question answering for crop health queries
  • AI advisory generation combining visual diagnosis with contextual knowledge
  • Confidence scoring and uncertainty quantification

Phase 6 — Prototype Development

  • Gradio/Streamlit web prototype for initial testing
  • FastAPI backend for scalable deployment
  • Future Flutter mobile application for field use
  • User testing with farming communities in Punjab and Sindh
